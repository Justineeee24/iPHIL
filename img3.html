SAGADA

TOURIST SPOTS

SAGADA'S HANGING COFFINS

High up on limestone cliffs are the Sugong Hanging Coffins. This burial practice is an ancient tradition still practiced by some of the people of Sagada. Suspending their deceased loved ones’ coffins up on mountain cliffs are said to bring their souls closer to heaven. The hanging coffins in Sagada can be seen on the road leading to the Sumaguing Cave entrance.

SUMAGUING CAVE

If you’re looking for an exciting adventure that will test your limits, Sumaguing Cave or Sumaging-Latipan-Lomyang Crystal Cave has got you covered. One of the most famous caves in the Philippines, it has the largest chamber built over the years, with natural rock formations spread within. When you go spelunking in Sumaguing Cave, you will be getting wet and muddy, so make sure you wear comfortable clothing. At certain points of the trek, you will need to go barefoot so you can get a better grip of the rock.

MT. KILTEPAN'S PEAK

IAt 1,636m, Mt. Kiltepan is one of the highest peaks in Sagada, and a great vantage point to watch the sunrise. Seeing the sun peek out from behind the Cordillera mountains and low-lying clouds is a surreal experience. To get to the mountain, you can book a round trip transport from the town proper.

FOODS

PINIKPIKAN

A traditional Ifugao dish made by lightly beating a native chicken before cooking it with salt and native herbs. The unique preparation method is believed to enhance the flavor.

ETAG

Etag is a native delicacy in the Cordillera Administrative Region (CAR) made of pork cured in salt and then sun-dried or smoked. The flavor of this delicacy may soon be enjoyed in the form of granules, cubes and liquid seasoning.

YOGHURT

Sagada is known for its homemade yoghurt, often served with fruits or honey. It is a refreshing and healthy snack, perfect for the cool climate.

PLACES TO STAY

ROCK INN AND CAFE

A quaint inn set amidst an orange orchard, offering comfortable rooms and an on-site restaurant serving local cuisine. Guests can also enjoy the unique experience of picking oranges.

KANIP-AW PINES VIEW LODGE

Known for its beautiful views of the surrounding mountains and comfortable accommodations. The lodge offers a range of rooms, from budget to mid-range options.

SAGADA HERITAGE VILLAGE

Offers traditional Ifugao huts and modern rooms, providing a blend of cultural immersion and comfort. The village setting offers a peaceful retreat with beautiful views.
