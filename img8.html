DINAGAT ISLANDS

TOURIST SPOTS

BITAOG BEACH

Bitaog Beach located in San Jose offers white sand and large rock formations. The rock formation in Bitaog Beach is a popular photography spot. In this beach, there are many activities that you can do, such as sunbathing, snorkeling, and swimming. No need to be afraid, the waves on this beach are safe. For your accommodation, you can consider Mt. Bagarabon Beach and Mountain Resort is located in Surigao del Norte, the closest island to Dinagat Island. Staying at this resort, you will get easy access to the beach, barbecues, picnics, and other exciting activities.

PANGABANGAN ISLAND

Pangabangan Island or Isla Aga is an amazing place in Cagdianao, Dinagat Island. This place could be your next tourist destination while on holiday on Dinagat Island. Pangabangan Island, also known as Isla Aga, is an amazing place in Cagdianao. This island is famous for its beautiful white sand beaches and turquoise sea water. If you spend your time on this island, snorkeling or diving is the best activity to do. Pangabangan Island offers beautiful coral reefs, colorful fish, turtles, and several reef sharks. You do not need to bring snorkeling equipment because many shop rentals can be found easily near the Island.

CAB-ILAN BEACH

The shape of Cab-ilan beach is very unique, like a crescent moon. This beach is located in Loreto, on the east coast of Dinagat Island. The cab-ilan Beach is famous for its white sand and clear seawater. Then, there are rock formations that add to the beauty of this place.

FOODS

CASSAVA - STAPLE FOOD

Did you know my favorite snacks cassava cake, tabirak (ginat-an), linidgid and puto are all made from Cassava? Did you know Cassava has lots of names? We know this as Jaba, kamuting kahoy, kamanting, balanghoy. But in other places they also have different names. Cassava is called mandioca, aipim, or macaxeira in Portuguese, mandio in Guaraní, maniok in Afrikaans and Rotuman, yuca or mandioca in Spanish, muhogo in Swahili,mogho in Gujarati, tapioka in Fijian, kappa or maracheeni in Malayalam, singkong or ubi kayu in Indonesian and Malay language, tugi in Ilocano, balinghoy in Tagalog, maniok in German, Danish and Czech, manyok in Haitian Creole, lumu in Kichwa, manioc in French, mannyokka in Sinhala, khoai mì, khoai sắn in Vietnamese, mianga in Kikuyu and cassave in Dutch.

SEA FOODS

Etag is a native delicacy in the Cordillera Administrative Region (CAR) made of pork cured in salt and then sun-dried or smoked. The flavor of this delicacy may soon be enjoyed in the form of granules, cubes and liquid seasoning.

INLAGIM

A traditional Ifugao dish made with boiled and seasoned pork or beef, often served during special occasions and rituals.
